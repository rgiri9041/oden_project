It consists of steamed rice and a cooked lentil or other pulses stew called dal. It is a staple food in these countries. Bhāt or chāwal means "boiled rice" in a number of Indo-Aryan languages. At higher elevations in Nepal, above 6,500 feet (2,000 m), where rice does not grow well, other grains such as maize, buckwheat, barley or millet may be substituted in a cooked preparation called dhindo or atho in Nepal. Bhat may be supplemented with roti in Nepal (rounds of unleavened bread).

Steps to prepare the dal vat tarkari

  1. Cook rice how you usually like it.
  2. Heat oil in a pot. Sweat the onion for a minute, until they start to turn slightly translucent. Add garlic, ginger, green chili and all the spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric). Stir for another 30 s.
  3. Add the lentils, salt and 800 mL water.
  4. Bring to a boil and simmer until the lentils are done and just about start falling apart.
  5. Adjust consistency by adding water if needed. This dal should be quite watery.
  6. Heat oil in a pan. If using whole cumin, add the seeds now and fry until they crackle. Add fenugreek seeds and let them develop some colour. If using powdered spices, add them below.
  7. Heat oil in a pan. If using whole cumin, add the seeds now and fry until they crackle. Add fenugreek seeds (if using) and dried chili (if using) and let both develop some colour. The chili will get soft. If using powdered spices, add them below.
  8. Serve dal, bhat, tarkari and sag with papads and pickled vegetables.
